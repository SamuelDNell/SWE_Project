Rutgers University — CS Department Course Catalog (Spring 2026)

CS 314: Principles of Programming Languages
Covers functional programming paradigms, type theory, and formal language design. Topics include lambda calculus, type inference, and interpreter construction. Meets Tuesdays and Thursdays at 10:20 AM in Hill Center Room 114. Professor Angela Rivera. Prerequisites: CS 112 and CS 205.

CS 416: Operating Systems Design
Explores process scheduling, virtual memory management, file system internals, and concurrency primitives. Students implement a custom shell and a memory allocator over the course of the semester. Taught by Professor David Kim. Final exam is scheduled for May 15th at 8:00 AM in SEC 111. Prerequisites: CS 211 and CS 214.

CS 440: Introduction to Artificial Intelligence
Introduces search algorithms, constraint satisfaction, probabilistic reasoning, and neural networks. Students complete five programming assignments, each building on the previous. Office hours are held every Monday from 2:00 PM to 4:00 PM. Prerequisites: CS 112, Math 250.

CS 461: Machine Learning
Covers supervised and unsupervised learning, gradient descent, regularization, decision trees, and deep learning. Textbook: "Pattern Recognition and Machine Learning" by Bishop. Midterm is worth 30% of the final grade. Professor Sarah Chen teaches two sections: one at 9:50 AM and one at 1:10 PM.
